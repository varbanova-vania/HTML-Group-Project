Once a mighty republic, Venice is now a magnet for tourists. It definitely has a special atmosphere. The architecture, the canals, the gondolas and the glorious history of the city. Through the small streets and canals you can discover charming details. Signs, street lamps, windows with shutters, balconies with flowers and bridges. It's great to get lost right here, between the walls, where history wafts around every corner. One immerses oneself in a bygone era.

One end of the canal leads into the lagoon near the Santa Lucia railway station, and the other end leads into the basin of San Marco; between them it makes a large inverted S shape through the central neighborhoods ( sestieri ) of Venice. It's long 3.8 km and is 30 to 90 m wide, with an average depth of 5 meters . The banks of the Grand Canal are lined with more than 170 buildings, most of which date from the 13th to the 18th centuries and showcase the wealth and art created by the Republic of Venice.

St. Mark's Square is located in the center of Venice and is its main square, also called "the official salon of Europe". For hundreds of years, the square has been the center of the political, religious and social life of the city.
